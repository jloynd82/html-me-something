Life After


A Summary

As I sit here and consider where I want this first assignment to go, I've come to a very reflective place. The past month has been one of transitions, so it seems only appropriate that I consider how my path is currently being shaped by my last two years of experience as an AmeriCorps service member in St. Louis. I've accomplished more than I can even describe -- both personally and professionally -- during my time volunteering, but I don't want to pretend that the choice to move or to self-motivate to continue service was an easy one. I worked long hours and was exhausted; I was sometimes beaten down; and, at the same time, not one ounce of me regrets any of the choices that I've made during it all, even if it did require me to completely revamp everything.

My time in STL as an AmeriCorps volunteer was undoubtedly representative of this list. So, now that the term is over, I find need to put down a few thoughts about my service. I found so much fulfillment in new experience but also grieved my old self, put away. These two years truly brought me to a space where I felt the drive to force change where federal, state, institutional, and organizational red tape inhibits and denounces self-advocacy. It's hard for me to move on from everything I've helped build. But, there's always next, which I'm calling now.
